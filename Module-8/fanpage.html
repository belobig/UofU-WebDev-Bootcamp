Daft Punk Tribute Page

Bio

Daft Punk are a French electronic music duo formed in 1993 by Guy-Manuel de Homem-Christo and Thomas Bangalter. They became pretty popular in the late 1990s as part of the French house movement and kept going from there. Their music combines elements of house music with funk, techno, disco, rock, and synthpop. They have pretty awesome visual stylization and disguises associated with their music, like their sweet helmets and gloves that make them look like robots. That's how they most often appear in public, and they don't grant interviews or appear on TV very often.

They were originally part of an indie rock band named Darlin' for a short while. After that group broke up, Bangalter and Homem-Christo experimented musically together with drum machines and synthesisers. Their first studio album Homework was released in 1997 with Virgin records, with very positive reviews. They had even more success with their next album Discovery in 2001 because of their prior release of hit singles "One More Time", "Digital Love", and "Harder, Better, Faster, Stronger". Their next album Human After All in 2005 had mixed reviews, but their singles "Robot Rock" and "Technologic" were pretty successful in the UK.

Daft Punk toured in 2006 and 2007 and released Alive 2007, their live album which won a Grammy Award for Best Electronic/Dance Album. Next, they composed the score for Disney's Tron: Legacy in 2010, also releasing its soundtrack that same year. They ended up leaving Virgin for Columbia Records in 2013, when they released their next album Random Access Memories, which the world pretty much loved. Their best single from the album, "Get Lucky", hit the top 10 charts in 32 countries, making it a huge international success.

In my opinion, Daft Punk are not only the originators of Electronic Dance Music, but also the best EDM group out there. They also have some pretty awesome merchandise. Check out their website for more info on the merch, if your interested: DaftPunk.com
